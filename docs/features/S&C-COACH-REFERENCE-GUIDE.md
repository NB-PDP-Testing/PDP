### What is This System?

This assessment tool helps you evaluate and track athlete fitness across **42 skills** in **6 categories**. Each skill is rated on a **1-5 scale** with age-appropriate benchmarks to show if athletes are on track for their level.

### Who is This For?

- **U14-U18 Athletes:** Building fitness foundation for GAA and sport

### Example: Aerobic Capacity Rating

| Rating | What It Looks Like |
|--------|-------------------|
| **1.0** | Struggles with 10+ minutes continuous effort, frequent breaks needed |
| **2.0** | Can sustain 20-30 minutes moderate effort with some discomfort |
| **3.0** | Can sustain 40-60 minutes at steady pace, adequate for training |
| **4.0** | Maintains high work rate 60+ minutes with minimal decline, good match fitness |
| **5.0** | Exceptional endurance, near-maximal effort for extended periods, elite level |

### How to Rate Between Levels

Use **half-points** (e.g., 3.5) when athlete is between two levels:
- **3.0** = Solid competent level
- **3.5** = Better than competent, approaching proficient
- **4.0** = Clearly proficient

## ğŸ¯ Level Definitions

### Recreational
- **Goal:** Fitness, health, enjoyment, participation
- **Hyrox:** Complete an event (60-90min adults)
- **GAA:** Club player fitness baseline
- **Training:** 2-3 sessions/week

### Competitive
- **Goal:** Performance improvement, competition success
- **Hyrox:** Sub-90min target (adults), age-group podiums
- **GAA:** County development panel fitness
- **Training:** 4-5 sessions/week

### Elite
- **Goal:** High performance, championships
- **Hyrox:** Sub-75min (adults), elite age group/open
- **GAA:** County senior panel fitness
- **Training:** 6+ sessions/week, structured program

## ğŸ“‹ Assessment Protocol

### Pre-Assessment Checklist

- â˜ Health screening completed (injury history, medical clearance)
- â˜ Athlete well-rested (no training 24h prior)
- â˜ Hydrated and fed (2-3 hours before)
- â˜ Appropriate clothing and footwear
- â˜ Equipment checked and ready

### Standardized Warm-Up (15 minutes)

1. **General Warm-Up (5 min):** Light jog, dynamic stretches
2. **Movement Prep (5 min):** Squats, lunges, arm circles, leg swings
3. **Specific Warm-Up (5 min):** Practice movements to be tested

### Testing Order (Minimize Fatigue Effects)

**Session 1 (45-60 minutes):**
1. Movement Quality (squat, hinge, mobility) - 10 min
2. Core & Stability (planks, balance) - 10 min
3. Speed & Agility (sprints, COD) - 15 min
4. Power Tests (jumps, throws) - 10 min

**Session 2 (45-60 minutes) - Different Day:**
5. Strength Tests (squat, push, pull) - 20 min
6. Hyrox Station Technique - 15 min
7. Endurance Test (last, most fatiguing) - 20 min

### Safety Guidelines

**Youth Athletes (U14-U16):**
- âš ï¸ Never max testing under age 14
- âš ï¸ Technique before load always
- âš ï¸ Progress gradually over 12-week blocks
- âš ï¸ Supervise all testing

**All Athletes:**
- ğŸ›‘ Stop if technique breaks down
- ğŸ›‘ Stop if pain (not discomfort) occurs
- ğŸ›‘ Adequate rest between tests (2-5 minutes)
- ğŸ›‘ Medical clearance for elite testing

### Recording Results

For each skill, record:
- **Rating (1-5):** Your assessment

- **Context:** Training/match/trial/formal review

## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips for Coaches

### Getting Accurate Ratings

âœ… **DO:**
- Observe multiple sessions before rating
- Test under various conditions (fresh vs fatigued)
- Use video analysis for movement quality
- Compare to benchmark descriptors, not other athletes
- Rate conservatively - easier to upgrade than downgrade

âŒ **DON'T:**
- Rate based on one session only
- Inflate ratings to "be nice"
- Compare athletes to each other
- Guess - if unsure, observe more
- Rate when athlete is injured or ill

### Common Rating Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Solution |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Over-rating** | Wanting athlete to feel good | Use descriptors objectively |
| **Under-rating** | High standards, comparing to elite | Use age-appropriate benchmarks |
| **Inconsistent** | Rating criteria changes | Review descriptors before each session |
| **Recency bias** | Latest session weighs too heavily | Consider 4-6 week average |

### Using with Athletes

**Feedback Best Practices:**
1. Start with **strengths** (what's on track or exceeding)
2. Frame **development areas** positively ("opportunity to improve")
3. Show **progress over time** (compare to previous assessments)
4. Link to **goals** (e.g., "improving this will help your Hyrox time")
5. Create **action plan** (2-3 focus areas max)

**Parent Communication:**
- Use benchmark status colors (easy visual)
- Explain what "on track" means for their age
- Emphasize long-term development over short-term results
- Share progress trends, not just current ratings
