## Skill Categories

### Category 1: Core Delivery Skills (4 skills)

| # | Skill Code | Skill Name | Description |
|---|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | `draw_shot` | Draw Shot | The fundamental shot - delivering the bowl with correct line and weight to reach the target (jack or position). The "bread and butter" of bowls. |
| 2 | `delivery_technique` | Delivery Technique | Biomechanics of the delivery: grip, stance, backswing, step, pendulum swing, release point, follow-through, and balance. |
| 3 | `line_control` | Line Control | Selecting and maintaining the correct aiming line to counteract bowl bias. Reading the green for forehand and backhand lines. |
| 4 | `weight_control` | Weight Control | Controlling delivery pace/velocity to achieve desired length. Weight errors account for 70%+ of poor bowls. The most difficult skill to master. |

### Category 2: Shot Variety (3 skills)

| # | Skill Code | Skill Name | Description |
|---|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 5 | `yard_on_shot` | Yard-On Shot | Controlled weighted shot with approximately one yard extra pace. Used to disturb the head, trail the jack, or displace opponent bowls. Requires narrower line than draw. |
| 6 | `drive_shot` | Drive / Firing Shot | Maximum-weight shot aimed at destroying the head, removing opponent bowls, or driving the jack to the ditch. The "heaviest" of weighted shots. |
| 7 | `positional_play` | Positional Play | Strategic bowl placement: back bowls (insurance), blockers (obstructing lines), rest shots, promotions. Building the head for tactical advantage. |

### Category 3: Game Intelligence (3 skills)

| # | Skill Code | Skill Name | Description |
|---|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 8 | `tactical_awareness` | Tactical Awareness | Shot selection based on head position, game state, ends remaining, and scoreboard. Shifting between draw/yard-on/running/drive as needed. |
| 9 | `green_reading` | Green Reading | Assessing green speed, surface conditions, slope, and bias behaviour. Adapting to weather, time of day, and indoor vs outdoor surfaces. |
| 10 | `mental_resilience` | Mental Resilience | Pre-shot routine, composure under pressure, managing frustration, concentration over long matches, and performing in championship situations. |

### Category 4: Social & Team Skills (2 skills)

| # | Skill Code | Skill Name | Description |
|---|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 11 | `team_play` | Team Play | Understanding and fulfilling position-specific roles (Lead, Second, Third, Skip). Communication, supporting teammates, and reading skip signals. |
| 12 | `etiquette_sportsmanship` | Etiquette & Sportsmanship | Mat protocol, rink conduct, respecting opponents, umpire decisions, Laws of the Sport knowledge, and representing club/country with distinction. |

## Rating Level Descriptors (1-5 Scale)

### Level 1 - Beginner
The bowler is new to the sport. Learning basic grip, stance, and delivery action. Minimal understanding of bias, line, or weight. Requires constant coaching guidance. Equivalent to first few coaching sessions / Play Bowls Activator taster sessions.

### Level 2 - Developing
The bowler has a functional delivery and understands basic concepts of bias, line, and weight. Can play a social game with assistance. Beginning to develop a pre-shot routine. Equivalent to completing a Level 1 coaching programme.

### Level 3 - Competent (Club Standard)
The bowler can participate competently in club competitions. Consistent delivery technique on both forehand and backhand. Good weight control on standard-length ends. Understands tactical basics and fulfils team position requirements. Equivalent to a regular club competitor.

### Level 4 - Advanced (County/Regional)
The bowler demonstrates refined skills across all shot types. Excellent green reading and weight adaptation. Strong tactical game management. Can represent club in inter-club and county-level competitions. Consistent under pressure with a reliable pre-shot routine.

### Level 5 - Elite (National/International)
The bowler operates at the highest level of the sport. Near-mechanical delivery consistency. Exceptional draw shot accuracy. Complete shot repertoire including precise weighted shots. Masterful tactical awareness and opponent reading. Represents at national or international level (e.g., World Bowls Championships, Commonwealth Games).

## Playing Positions

Lawn bowls team formats include Pairs (2), Triples (3), and Fours (4). Each position has specific responsibilities:

| Position | Role | Key Skills Emphasised |
|----------|------|----------------------|
| **Lead** | Sets the head, places the mat, delivers the jack | Draw shot accuracy, consistency, setting pace |
| **Second** | Builds on lead's work, adds to the head | Draw shot, yard-on, positional play |
| **Third** | Tactical role, measures disputed shots | All shot types, tactical awareness, communication with skip |
| **Skip** | Team captain, directs play, bowls last | Complete shot repertoire, tactical mastery, leadership |

## Development Pathway (Based on Bowls Canada LTAD)

| Stage | Description | Focus |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Active Start** | Introduction to bowls through taster sessions | Fun, basic motor skills, handling a bowl |
| **FUNdamentals** | Play Bowls 4 Fun programme | Enjoyment, basic delivery, understanding bias |
| **Learn to Bowl** | Structured coaching (Coach Bowls Level 1) | Delivery technique, grip, stance, basic draw |
| **Train to Bowl** | Regular practice and club competition | Technical refinement, all shot types, team play |
| **Train to Compete** | County/regional competition focus | Tactical development, match play, mental skills |
| **Train to Win** | National-level competition | Elite performance, pressure management, opposition analysis |
| **Compete to Win** | International representation | Peak performance, World Bowls / Commonwealth Games |
| **Active for Life** | Lifelong participation | Health, social, veteran competitions |
