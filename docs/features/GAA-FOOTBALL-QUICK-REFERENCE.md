### âœ… DO:

- Assess in game situations (primary)
- Test fresh AND fatigued
- Rate conservatively
- Use descriptors, not comparisons
- Document conditions

## ğŸ® GAMES-BASED ASSESSMENT

**Priority Order:**
1. **Match play** (competitive games)
2. **Conditioned games** (game-like with constraints)
3. **Small-sided games** (reduced numbers)
4. **Technical drills** (beginners only)

**Why?** Drill perfection â‰  Game effectiveness

## ğŸ”„ BILATERAL DEVELOPMENT

**Critical for Higher Ratings:**
- Level 1-2: Strong side only
- Level 3: Adequate both sides
- Level 4: Strong both sides
- Level 5: Equal both sides

**Research:** Left-sided players develop bilaterality faster than right-sided players

**Assessment:** Does player avoid weaker side in matches? If yes â†’ not Level 4+

## âš¡ SPECIAL NOTES

### Free Taking
- No official accuracy standards exist in GAA
- Use percentages as guidelines: 70% (Rec), 80% (Comp), 90% (Elite)
- Assess over multiple sessions under pressure

### Tactical Skills
- Develop slower than technical skills
- U8-U10 typically 1.0-1.5 (learning concepts)
- Games-based coaching accelerates development

### Laterality
- Separate assessment OR modifier to other skills
- Elite expectation: equal proficiency both sides
- Track Left/Right Side skills to monitor bilateral progress

### Age Transitions
- U12â†’U14 particularly challenging (full field, increased physicality)
- Don't immediately downgrade during transition
- Assess over 4-6 weeks after move-up
