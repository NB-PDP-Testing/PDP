## PRODUCT BRIEF

**PlayerARC** (Player Development Platform) is a B2B SaaS platform for youth sports clubs combining:

- **AI-powered voice notes for coaches** — record training observations → automatic transcription → AI extracts structured insights → coach approves in one tap → sent to parents
- **Player Development Passport** — skills assessment, injury tracking, goals, benchmarks, full history — travels with the player across every sport they play
- **Parent engagement automation** — AI-generated development summaries pushed to parents after every session
- **Multi-sport player graph** — player-centred architecture connecting coaches across GAA, rugby, soccer, athletics, Irish dancing, S&C
- **Benchmark knowledge graph** — empirically grounded benchmarks built from real coaching observations at scale; gets more accurate with every club that joins
- **Club admin** — bulk import, team management, roles, GDPR consent flows

**Built in Ireland. GDPR-native. EU-first.**

**Key differentiator:** The only platform that is player-centred (not club-centred) and builds a data moat with every session recorded. Every competitor is a single-sport, single-club silo.

**Business model:** B2B SaaS — €500–€1,200/club/year or €10–15/coach/month. Data licensing (Year 3+). API/integrations (Year 5+).

There is no joined-up picture of that child's development — not for the parent, not for the coaches, not for the child. 70% of youth athletes quit sport by age 13. Fragmented, disconnected development is a primary driver.

**Closer:** Every tool on the market solves Problem 1 or Problem 3 in isolation. Nobody has touched Problem 4. That is the structural gap PlayerARC was built to fill.

### SLIDE 4: HOW IT WORKS

**Headline:** 60 seconds of coach voice. A lifetime of player development.

**Subheadline:** The simplest coaching tool ever built — and the richest data source in youth sport.

**Step 1: Coach speaks**
After any session, the coach records a voice note — 30 to 90 seconds. No typing. No forms. No login friction. Works from the car park.

**Step 2: AI processes**
PlayerARC transcribes, categorises insights by skill domain, flags potential injury signals, and structures the note against the player's existing development profile. Sport-specific benchmarks surface automatically: *"Ciarán's passing accuracy has moved from the 45th to the 68th percentile for U10 GAA midfielders."*

**Step 3: Coach approves**
One screen. Coach reads the AI summary, adjusts if needed, taps approve. Median time: under 90 seconds. GDPR audit trail created automatically.

**Step 4: Parent receives — and the passport grows**
The parent gets a clear, personalised development update in plain language. But this is also where PlayerARC diverges from every other tool:
- That observation is permanently recorded in the player's Development Passport
- If this player plays a second sport and their other coach has consent access, the insight is visible to them
- The benchmark knowledge graph absorbs one more real-world data point — making every subsequent benchmark more accurate for every club on the platform

**Closer:** Every voice note a coach records makes PlayerARC smarter — for that player, for that club, and for every club that joins after them.

## STANDALONE SLIDE: NETWORK EFFECTS & DATA MOAT

*(Appendix — use with any audience where the data thesis needs full treatment)*

**Headline:** The data moat is structurally impossible to replicate. Here is why.

**Subheadline:** PlayerARC is a SaaS tool that becomes a data platform. The data platform is what makes it irreplaceable.

### The Benchmark Problem in Youth Sport Today

Youth sports development benchmarks fall into three categories:
- **Academic** — derived from elite programs, not representative of grassroots reality
- **Anecdotal** — what a given coach thinks an average U10 looks like
- **Absent** — GAA, rugby, Irish dancing have no official grassroots 1–5 rating scales anywhere

PlayerARC is building the fourth category: **empirically grounded, real-world, grassroots-native benchmarks.** Not what academics think a 75th-percentile U10 GAA midfielder looks like. What coaches across 1,000 clubs, watching 50,000 players, have actually observed — structured by AI into a consistent, comparable format.

### Why This Cannot Be Retrofitted

Benchmark data has three requirements: **volume** (thousands of observations), **time** (multiple seasons to track progression), and **consistency** (same skill taxonomy applied across all clubs from day one).

A competitor starting today would need to:
1. Sign thousands of clubs (years of sales effort)
2. Wait multiple full seasons for longitudinal data to accumulate
3. Apply an identical benchmark framework from day one — impossible to retrofit onto historical free-text notes

They cannot buy real coaching history. They cannot copy it. They can only start from zero and wait. By the time they have 10,000 clubs worth of data, PlayerARC has 10 years of it.

### The Flywheel in Full

```
More clubs join
      ↓
More sessions recorded → richer observations
      ↓
Benchmark dataset grows → percentile accuracy improves
      ↓
Coaches get insight they couldn't generate alone
      ↓
Platform more valuable → retention up → word-of-mouth accelerates
      ↓
Governing bodies see dataset → want partnership
      ↓
Governing bodies promote PlayerARC to member clubs
      ↓
More clubs join ↑ (back to top)
```

Every turn of the flywheel makes the next turn faster. Every club that joins makes the platform more valuable for every club already on it.
