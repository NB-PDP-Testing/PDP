# PlayerARC — Adult Player Lifecycle: Consolidated Product Gaps

**Purpose:** A single-source-of-truth reference for the product owner covering every identified gap across all 8 Adult Player Lifecycle phases. Covers resolved items (for traceability), open gaps (in industry comparison but not yet fully addressed in a PRD story), and missing features (absent from all PRDs).

**How to read this document:**
- **Status:** ✅ Resolved | 🔶 Open (in PRD/comparison doc, no story yet) | ❌ Missing (not in any PRD)
- **Priority:** 🔴 Must (legal requirement, safety, or major competitive gap) | 🟡 Should (strong industry standard or significant UX gap) | 🟢 Could (differentiating or emerging feature)
- **Effort:** Low (≤3 stories) | Medium (4–7 stories) | High (8+ stories, new module, or third-party integration)
- **Source phases:** P1–P8 refer to the eight Adult Player Lifecycle phases

**Research basis:** Fresh industry research (Feb 2026) + synthesis of all 8 PRDs + INDUSTRY-COMPARISON.md. Platforms reviewed: Kitman Labs, Catapult, Teamworks/SmartAbase, Hudl, SportsEngine, TeamSnap, Sportlyzer, FitrWoman/Orreco, Polar, AthleteMonitoring, Trace, Pixellot, aiScout, Tonsser. Regulatory frameworks: GDPR (Irish DPC guidance), GDPR Article 9, Article 17, Article 20, Article 30, Article 33/34, Article 35 (DPIA), IOC STROBE-SIIS 2020, COPPA 2025, WCAG 2.1 AA, FIFPRO Player Data Rights Charter.

| B-01 | PWA installability + native push notifications | P1, P4, P8 | IndexedDB offline sync for wellness check-in (P4); WhatsApp/SMS wellness reminders (P8); no service worker, no home-screen install, no app push | Kitman Labs, Catapult: native iOS/Android apps with push. | B-04 | Session RPE entry (post-session exertion rating) | P4 | Daily wellness check-in (mood, sleep, energy, motivation, physical feeling) at a fixed daily time | Session RPE (Borg CR-10 or modified 1–10 scale × session minutes = session load) is the #1 evidence-based metric for internal training load; present on **all** major platforms (Kitman Labs, Catapult, AthleteMonitoring, Teamworks AMS); asked by athlete after each training session, not daily | 🔶 Open | 🟡 Should | Medium | Most clinically valid single metric in sports science — used in published injury research; distinct from daily wellness (session-triggered, not time-triggered); enables ACWR calculation (B-09); doesn't require wearables; relatively simple UX (one 1–10 question + session duration) | Requires a training session to attach the RPE to — needs B-14 (session log) first; coaches need to mark sessions as complete for the prompt to fire; adds friction if clubs don't log training sessions in the platform |
| B-05 | Injury triage — medical staff notification for severe injuries | P5 | Self-reported injury appears in coach view with "Player-reported" badge; coach is notified | Teamworks, Kitman Labs: severe self-reported injury triggers notification to physio/medical staff first, not just the coach; stage-gate RTP (Return to Play) progression with medical sign-off | 🔶 Open | 🟡 Should | Low | Clinical safety improvement — severe injuries require medical assessment before coaching response; aligns with Irish Sport and UK Sport safeguarding standards; low implementation cost (notification routing change based on severity threshold + a designated medical contact per org) | Requires a designated "medical contact" per org; if none is configured, notification has nowhere to go (fallback to admin is needed); does not need a full medical_staff role — a "medical contact" field on org settings is sufficient at grassroots level |
| B-06 | Progress visualisation — radar/spider chart | P5 | Skill rating list with trend arrows (↑ ↓ =) | Kitman Labs, Catapult: radar/spider charts showing the player's overall profile shape across dimensions; visually shows relative strengths and weaknesses at a glance | 🔶 Open | 🟢 Could | Low | Recharts `RadarChart` component is already available in the project — single component addition; visually compelling and immediately conveys profile shape vs. numbers in a list; could be implemented as secondary view alongside the numerical list | Radar charts can mislead (area scaling, axis ordering both affect perception of the data); some users find them harder to interpret than numbers; not a functional gap — trend arrows serve the same purpose differently |
| B-07 | Role audit log | P6 | `parentChildAuthorizationLogs` (P7) for child access changes only; no system-wide audit log | Kitman Labs, Teamworks, Catapult maintain immutable audit logs for all data access and modification — required for GDPR Article 30 (Records of Processing Activities) and club governance investigations | 🔶 Open | 🔴 Must | Medium | GDPR Article 30 compliance — DPO must be able to produce a record of who accessed what and when; enables investigation of data incidents; deters inappropriate data access; builds trust with clubs that have formal data governance requirements | High write volume at scale — every mutation requires an additional audit insert, increasing DB cost and latency; audit log itself must not contain PII values (log action/table/ID, not the data); a lightweight `auditEvents` table (userId, role, action, targetTable, targetId, timestamp, orgId) covers the requirement without over-engineering |
| B-08 | ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio) alerts | P4 | 7-day wellness sparkline for coaches | Catapult, Kitman Labs: ACWR = 7-day acute load ÷ 28-day chronic load. "Danger zone" > 1.5. Used as the primary evidence-based injury risk signal. Threshold alerts sent to coaches/medical staff. | 🔶 Open | 🟡 Should | Medium | Most widely-used injury prevention metric in sports science; differentiating for grassroots clubs; coach-facing load management alerts are a core Catapult/Kitman Labs value proposition; would require session RPE (B-04) and session log (C-01) as prerequisites | ACWR requires ≥28 days of consistent sRPE data to be valid — new players or inconsistent loggers produce misleading scores; academic debate on ACWR methodology (rolling vs. EWMA); if session data is incomplete, ACWR is potentially harmful (false reassurance or false alarm) — must require B-04 and C-01 first |
| B-12 | COPPA verifiable parental consent (US deployment) | P7 | Parent-grant flow satisfies GDPR Article 8 for Irish/EU deployment; no COPPA-compliant identity verification | COPPA 2025 (effective June 2025, compliance by April 2026): three new verifiable consent methods — knowledge-based questions, facial recognition of government ID, text-plus SMS. "Parent logs in and clicks" is NOT a recognised method. GitHub issue #568 tracks this. | 🔶 Open (deferred) | 🟢 Could | High | Required for US deployment; COPPA 2025 is active law; knowledge-based Q services (LexisNexis, Experian) are commercially available as APIs | Irish/EU deployment is fully covered by the parent-grant flow (GDPR Article 8); COPPA is US-only; knowledge-based Q / facial ID / text-plus services are expensive third-party integrations; appropriately deferred to US-expansion phase — not relevant for current Irish/EU target market |
