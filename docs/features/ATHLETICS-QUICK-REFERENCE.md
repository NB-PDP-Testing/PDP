## Core Skills by Category

### 1. Running Mechanics (Applies to ALL events)

| Skill | Level 1 | Level 3 | Level 5 |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Posture & Alignment** | Looking down, inconsistent | Good alignment, self-corrects | Perfect, automatic, world-class |
| **Arm Action** | Crosses midline, tense | Good forward-back action | Perfect arm-leg coordination |
| **Hip Extension** | Minimal extension | Strong drive, full extension | Maximal power, elite force |
| **Knee Drive** | Minimal lift, shuffle | Appropriate for speed | Perfect front-side mechanics |
| **Foot Strike** | Heavy heel strike | Midfoot, near center of mass | Biomechanically optimal |
| **Ground Contact Time** | Long, inefficient | Reasonably quick | Exceptionally quick, maximal propulsion |
| **Stride Length** | Short or overstriding | Appropriate for speed | Optimal for event |
| **Stride Frequency** | Low cadence | 170-180 spm | 180+ spm (distance), event-optimal |
| **Core Stability** | Minimal engagement | Adequate stability | World-class stability, zero energy leak |
| **Ankle Control** | Poor stiffness | Good reactivity | Elite stiffness and reactivity |

### 2. Speed & Power Skills

| Skill | Event Focus | Level 1 | Level 3 | Level 5 |
|-------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Acceleration** | All events | Weak drive, poor lean | Powerful, good mechanics | World-class explosive ability |
| **Maximum Velocity** | Sprints | Low top speed | Good for club level | Elite top-end speed |
| **Speed Endurance** | Sprints, Mid-Dist | Ties up early | Maintains form when tired | World-class lactate tolerance |
| **Power Output** | Sprints | Limited force | Strong force production | Exceptional explosive power |
| **Starting Blocks** | Sprints | Poor reaction/drive | Competent block starts | Perfect block clearance |

### 3. Endurance & Physiological

| Skill | Event Focus | Level 1 | Level 3 | Level 5 |
|-------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Aerobic Capacity** | Mid/Long Dist | Low VO2 max | Good club-level aerobic base | World-class VO2 max |
| **Lactate Threshold** | Mid/Long Dist | Low LT | Solid threshold for club | Elite LT, 85-90% VO2 max |
| **Running Economy** | All events | Inefficient | Good economy | World-class efficiency |
| **Lactate Tolerance** | Sprints, Mid-Dist | Poor tolerance | Can sustain effort | Elite tolerance, minimal degradation |
| **Recovery Ability** | All events | Slow recovery | Adequate recovery | Superior recovery capacity |

## Event-Specific Skill Requirements

### Sprints (100m, 200m, 400m)

**Priority Skills:**
1. Acceleration Ability (Level 3+ for competitive)
2. Maximum Velocity (Level 3+ for competitive)
3. Starting Blocks Technique (Level 3+ for competitive)
4. Speed Endurance (critical for 200m/400m)
5. Power Output (Level 3+ for competitive)
6. Running Mechanics (all 10 fundamental skills)
7. Cornering Technique (200m/400m)

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- First 30m acceleration
- Maximum velocity maintenance
- Minimal deceleration in final phase
- Block reaction time <0.15s (elite)

### Middle Distance (800m, 1500m)

**Priority Skills:**
1. Aerobic Capacity (Level 3+ for competitive)
2. Lactate Threshold (Level 3+ for competitive)
3. Running Economy (Level 3+ for competitive)
4. Pacing Judgment (critical for race execution)
5. Race Strategy & Tactics (Level 3+ for competitive)
6. Lactate Tolerance (especially 800m)
7. Running Mechanics (all 10 fundamental skills)

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- Even pacing or controlled surges
- VO2 max >60 ml/kg/min (elite males), >55 ml/kg/min (elite females)
- Lactate threshold at 80-85% VO2 max (elite)
- Effective kick in final 200-400m

### Long Distance (3000m, 5000m, 10000m)

**Priority Skills:**
1. Aerobic Capacity (Level 4+ for elite)
2. Running Economy (Level 4+ for elite)
3. Lactate Threshold (Level 4+ for elite)
4. Pacing Judgment (Level 4+ for elite)
5. Mental Toughness (endurance events are mental)
6. Recovery Ability (high training volume)
7. Running Mechanics (efficiency critical)

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- VO2 max >70 ml/kg/min (elite males), >65 ml/kg/min (elite females)
- Training volume 80-150 km/week (elite)
- 80%+ training at low intensity
- Even pacing or controlled racing

### Hurdles (100mH, 110mH, 400mH)

**Priority Skills:**
1. Hurdle Clearance Technique (Level 3+ for competitive)
2. Acceleration Ability (Level 3+ for competitive)
3. Maximum Velocity (Level 3+ for competitive)
4. Speed Endurance (especially 400mH)
5. Running Mechanics (all fundamental skills)
6. Rhythm & Consistency (3-step pattern for sprint hurdles)
7. Flexibility (hip mobility for trail leg)

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- Consistent 3-step pattern between hurdles (sprint hurdles)
- 13-15 strides to first hurdle
- Minimal clearance height
- Rhythm maintenance when fatigued (400mH)

## Progression Pathways

### From Developmental to Competitive (Typical Timeline: 2-4 years)

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Consistency:** Regular training attendance
2. **Technical Foundation:** Master fundamental running mechanics (Level 2→3)
3. **Physical Development:** Build aerobic base and general strength
4. **Event Exploration:** Try multiple events before specializing
5. **Competition Experience:** Local and regional meets

**Milestones:**
- Training 3-4x per week consistently
- Skill levels reach Level 3 in core mechanics
- Performance approaching competitive standards for age
- Understanding of training principles developing

### From Competitive to Elite (Typical Timeline: 4-8 years)

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Event Specialization:** Commit to primary event(s)
2. **Technical Refinement:** Perfect event-specific skills (Level 3→4)
3. **Training Volume/Intensity:** Increase training load progressively
4. **Physiological Development:** Maximize aerobic/anaerobic capacity
5. **Mental Skills:** Develop championship mindset
6. **Competition Strategy:** Learn tactical racing

**Milestones:**
- Training 5-8x per week with periodization
- Skill levels reach Level 4 in event-specific areas
- Performance at national age-group standards
- Demonstrating potential for international competition

### From Elite to World-Class (Typical Timeline: 4-8 years)

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Biomechanical Optimization:** Perfect all technical elements (Level 4→5)
2. **Maximizing Genetic Potential:** Peak physiological development
3. **Championship Performance:** Deliver under pressure
4. **Recovery Management:** Optimize training adaptation
5. **Professional Mindset:** Year-round high-performance lifestyle
6. **International Competition:** World Championships, Olympics

**Milestones:**
- Training 6-10x per week, year-round periodization
- Skill levels reach Level 5 in key areas
- Performance at international standards
- Competing at World Championships, Olympics

## Age-Appropriate Skill Introduction

### U10-U12: Foundation Phase
**Introduce:**
- All basic running mechanics (fun, game-based)
- Multi-event exposure (try everything!)
- Simple sprint and distance races
- Basic relay handoffs
- Introduction to hurdles (low height, fun)

**Avoid:**
- Heavy strength training
- High-volume endurance (max 30-40 min runs)
- Advanced technical drilling
- Excessive competition

### U14-U16: Development Phase
**Introduce:**
- Event-specific training
- Structured periodization
- Age-appropriate strength training
- Physiological testing (basic)
- Tactical racing concepts
- Advanced hurdle technique

**Build:**
- Technical refinement in chosen events
- Aerobic base development
- Competitive experience at regional/national level

### U18-Senior: Performance Phase
**Introduce:**
- High-volume/intensity training
- Advanced physiological testing
- Biomechanical analysis
- Mental skills training
- International competition (if elite)

**Maximize:**
- Event-specific technical perfection
- Physiological capacities (VO2 max, LT, economy)
- Championship performance skills
- Recovery and adaptation strategies

## Common Technical Issues and Fixes

### Running Mechanics

| Issue | Common in... | Fix Focus |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| Looking down | Beginners (Level 1-2) | "Eyes on horizon," video feedback |
| Overstriding | All levels | "Quick feet," increase cadence 5-10% |
| Tense shoulders | Stressed athletes | "Relax hands," "potato chip" cue |
| Sitting back | Sprinters, tired runners | "Lean from ankles," core work |
| Excessive vertical motion | Inefficient runners | "Run forward not up," drills |
| Poor arm action | Beginners, fatigued | "Drive elbows back," arm drills |

### Event-Specific Issues

| Issue | Event | Fix Focus |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Slow starts | Sprints | Block practice, reaction drills, first-step power |
| Tying up late | 400m, 800m | Lactate tolerance training, relaxation cues |
| Poor pacing | Middle/Long Distance | Threshold runs, internal awareness, race practice |
| Hurdle hitting | Hurdles | Lead leg drill, trail leg snap, flexibility |
| Wide on curve | 200m/400m | Cornering drills, inside lane practice |
| No finishing kick | Middle Distance | Speed endurance, confidence, commitment |

## Assessment Quick Guide

### How to Use Benchmarks

1. **Identify Athlete Profile:**
   - Age group (U10-Senior)
   - Gender (Male/Female)
   - Competitive level (Developmental/Competitive/Elite)
   - Event specialization (Sprints/Mid-Dist/Long-Dist/Hurdles)

2. **Find Expected Level:**
   - Look up in matrix above
   - This is the target level for that skill

3. **Assess Current Level:**
   - Observe athlete in training/competition
   - Use technical indicators from benchmarks
   - Rate on 1-5 scale

4. **Compare:**
   - **Below expected:** Priority development area
   - **At expected:** Continue maintaining/refining
   - **Above expected:** Strength to leverage

5. **Plan Development:**
   - Use progression paths from benchmarks
   - Focus on skills below expected level
   - Maintain strengths

## Key Coaching Cues by Skill

### Running Mechanics
- **Posture:** "Run tall," "Eyes up," "Lean from ankles"
- **Arms:** "Drive elbows back," "Potato chips" (relaxed hands)
- **Hips:** "Push the ground away," "Triple extension"
- **Knees:** "High knees" (sprints), "Economical lift" (distance)
- **Feet:** "Quick feet," "Hot coals," "Land underneath you"

### Speed Development
- **Acceleration:** "Aggressive lean," "Drive, drive, drive"
- **Max Velocity:** "Tall and relaxed," "Fast arms, fast legs"
- **Speed Endurance:** "Maintain form when tired," "Stay relaxed"

### Endurance Development
- **Aerobic:** "Conversational pace," "Build the base"
- **Threshold:** "Comfortably hard," "Controlled discomfort"
- **Economy:** "Smooth and efficient," "No wasted motion"

### Event-Specific
- **Hurdles:** "Attack the hurdle," "Snap the trail leg"
- **Relay:** "Stick!" (handoff command), "Hit your mark"
- **Finishing:** "Accelerate through," "Lean at line"
